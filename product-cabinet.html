The Science of Selves

Internal Family Systems (IFS) is an evidence-based psychotherapy approach developed by Dr. Richard Schwartz in the 1980s. It recognizes the mind as naturally multiple - a system of interacting subpersonalities each with valuable qualities.

IFS therapy helps individuals access their core Self - the calm, curious, compassionate center of consciousness that can heal wounded parts and restore inner harmony.

Cabinet of Selves adapts these clinical insights into a structured self-exploration tool, helping you map your internal system without requiring therapy.

Psychological Guidance

Answers to your therapeutic questions

Is this a replacement for therapy?

No. Cabinet of Selves is designed as a self-help tool based on therapeutic principles, but it doesn't replace professional mental health care. For clinical issues, we recommend working with an IFS-trained therapist.

How does this differ from journaling?

While journaling is valuable, our tool provides structured clinical protocols from IFS therapy. The mapping and visualization tools help you see systemic relationships between parts that traditional journaling might miss.

Is my psychological data secure?

Absolutely. We use end-to-end encryption and never share your data. Your psychological work remains completely private, with options to export or delete all information at any time.

What if I encounter difficult emotions?

The tool includes grounding exercises and pacing protocols. However, if you encounter overwhelming material, we recommend pausing and consulting a mental health professional before continuing your work.

Can I use this alongside therapy?

Many therapists recommend using Cabinet of Selves to reinforce clinical work. You can share your maps and insights with your therapist to enhance your sessions. Always follow your therapist's guidance.
