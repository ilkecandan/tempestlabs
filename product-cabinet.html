Understanding Internal Family Systems

A compassionate approach to self-discovery and healing

If you've ever thought, "Part of me feels one way, but another part of me feels something completely different," you're already understanding what Internal Family Systems (IFS) is about.

IFS is a therapeutic approach that helps you get to know the many parts that make up your internal world‚Äîlike unique voices within you, each with their own stories, feelings, and needs.

Why Do We Have Parts?

Life shapes us. Experiences, both big and small, leave marks on how we see ourselves and the world. To survive difficult moments, we naturally develop parts that step in to protect us.

What is the Self?

In IFS, Self is the core of who you are‚Äîthe calm, confident, compassionate essence beneath all the noise of your parts.

How to Begin Your IFS Journey

1

Recognize your parts

Pay attention to your inner dialogue. When you feel anxious, sad, or insecure, ask yourself: "Is there a part of me that feels this way?"

2

Be curious, not critical

Be curious about your parts instead of judging them. Question: "What are you trying to protect me from?"

Your Inner Parts

Explore and understand the different aspects of yourself

Protector

This part works hard to keep you safe from emotional pain, sometimes through avoidance or control.

Overly Confident

This part compensates for insecurities by projecting an exaggerated sense of self-assurance.

Grumpy Part

This part emerges when you're tired or overwhelmed, expressing frustration on your behalf.

Nurturer

This part wants to take care of others, sometimes at the expense of your own needs.
