The Science of Selves

Internal Family Systems (IFS) is an evidence-based psychotherapy approach developed by Dr. Richard Schwartz in the 1980s. It recognizes the mind as naturally multiple - a system of interacting subpersonalities each with valuable qualities.

IFS therapy helps individuals access their core Self - the calm, curious, compassionate center of consciousness that can heal wounded parts and restore inner harmony.

Cabinet of Selves adapts these clinical insights into a structured self-exploration tool, helping you map your internal system without requiring therapy.

Important: This is not a replacement for therapy. We recommend working with an IFS-trained therapist for clinical issues.

The Power of Visualization

Visualization is a powerful therapeutic tool that bridges the gap between conscious and unconscious processes, allowing for deeper self-understanding and integration.

Self-Concept Exploration

Visualization allows individuals to mentally simulate different scenarios, aiding in the understanding and integration of various self-aspects. By creating mental images of our internal parts, we can engage with them more directly and compassionately.

Therapeutic Techniques

Practices like guided imagery can help individuals confront and reconcile conflicting parts of their identity, fostering a cohesive self-concept. Research shows that visualization activates the same neural networks as actual experiences, making it a potent tool for psychological change.

Psychological Guidance

Answers to your therapeutic questions

Is this a replacement for therapy?

No. Cabinet of Selves is designed as a self-help tool based on therapeutic principles, but it doesn't replace professional mental health care. For clinical issues, we recommend working with an IFS-trained therapist.

Important Note: We are not therapists. This tool is meant to complement therapy, not replace it.

How does this differ from journaling?

While journaling is valuable, our tool provides structured clinical protocols from IFS therapy. The mapping and visualization tools help you see systemic relationships between parts that traditional journaling might miss.

We also use DeepSeek's AI API to provide insights while maintaining your privacy - all processing happens locally on your device.

Is my psychological data secure?

Absolutely. We use a local storage only approach - your data never leaves your device. There are no user profiles or accounts to create. You can export your work as PDF for your records or to share with your therapist.

For added security, the app includes an option to completely wipe all local data at any time.

What if I encounter difficult emotions?

The tool includes grounding exercises and pacing protocols. However, if you encounter overwhelming material, we recommend pausing and consulting a mental health professional before continuing your work.

Remember: This is a self-exploration tool, not a therapeutic intervention. Proceed at your own pace.

Can I use this alongside therapy?

Many therapists recommend using Cabinet of Selves to reinforce clinical work. You can share your maps and insights with your therapist to enhance your sessions. Always follow your therapist's guidance.

We provide PDF export specifically for this purpose - to facilitate communication between you and your mental health professional.
